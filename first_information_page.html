The First Running Shoe

the beginning of the running shoe

The first shoe to ever be specifically made for running and athletisism was made in the 1832 by Wait Webster, called the Plimsolls, which utilized rubber soles and a canvas upper. However, the shoe was mostly worn by children.

The image shown above is the spiked version of the plimsolls that was created in 1852 by Joseph William Foster
